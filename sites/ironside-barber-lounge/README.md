# Ironside Barber Lounge

Downtown LA's chair for the sharpest men in the room.

_Generated by the Second Brain website_creator_agent on 2026-07-20._

## Preview locally
```bash
bash serve.sh          # then open http://localhost:8080
# or: bash serve.sh 9001   to use a different port
```

## Pages
- `index.html` — Cinematic scroll journey from the city street to the barber chair, then convert with services teaser, social proof, and a strong booking CTA.
- `services.html` — Full menu of services and pricing tiers so clients can choose and commit to a booking.
- `visit.html` — Give directions, hours, parking, and contact info, and drive final booking action.

## Design system
- **Aesthetic:** A late-night downtown LA barbershop rendered in charcoal and gunmetal, lit by warm brass sconces and a single neon sign glow. Think architectural noir photography, brushed steel textures, and typographic confidence — headlines cut like a straight razor, generous black space, and metallic accent lines that guide the eye from street to chair.
- **Fonts:** Oswald (headings) / Karla (body) — loaded via Google Fonts.
- **Palette:** bg: #131110, surface: #1d1a18, text: #ede7e0, muted: #a89b8d, primary: #b8863f, accent: #d4a24e, border: #332d28, on_primary: #131110
- All tokens live as CSS variables in `styles.css`.

## Structure
- `*.html` — the pages
- `styles.css` — the shared, hand-tuned stylesheet (single source of design truth)
- `effects.css` — guaranteed motion/cinematic layer, loaded after styles.css (kept separate so it always works)
- `main.js` — progressive-enhancement script (mobile nav, scroll-reveal animations, FAQ accordion, sticky-nav shadow)
- `serve.sh` — local preview server


## Cinematic intro
The home page opens with a scroll-scrubbed cinematic intro (6 scenes) that pins full-screen
and "travels in" as you scroll, then releases into the content. Scenes:

1. **THE CITY NEVER SITS STILL** — ideal footage: Wide aerial drone shot of downtown LA at dusk, streetlights flickering on, traffic streaking below skyscrapers
2. **SLOW DOWN HERE** — ideal footage: Street-level tracking shot down a quiet alley, brick and steel buildings, IRONSIDE neon glow reflected in a puddle
3. **KNOCK TWICE** — ideal footage: Close-up macro shot of a heavy brass door handle on a black steel door, warm sconce light spilling from the crack
4. **WELCOME TO THE CHAIR** — ideal footage: Interior tracking shot gliding past vintage barber chairs, exposed brick, brass sconces, straight razors glinting on a leather strop
5. **PRECISION, NEAT** — ideal footage: Slow-motion close-up of a barber's straight razor gliding along a jawline, steam towel steaming beside the chair
6. **TAKE YOUR SEAT** — ideal footage: Chair spins slowly to face camera under a spotlight, empty and waiting, brass IRONSIDE emblem glowing on the headrest

**These scenes use gradient placeholders — swap in real footage to make it truly cinematic.**
In `index.html`, find each `.cinema__bg` and set a real image/video still:
```html
<div class="cinema__bg" style="--scene-img: url('scene1.jpg');" ...></div>
```
Drop `scene1.jpg` (etc.) into this folder. Each scene already carries the ideal-shot description in an
HTML comment right above it. The scroll zoom, pinning, and headline timing all keep working unchanged.

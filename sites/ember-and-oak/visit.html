Parking & Transit

Street Parking

Free after 6 PM along Birchwood Lane and 5th Street. Arrive earlier and you may need a block or two of patience — this neighborhood fills up fast on weekends.

The Lot Behind Us

Our small back lot (entrance off 5th) has 14 spaces for guests. First come, first served — no valet, no fuss.

Bus & Bike

The 22 and 40 lines both stop at Birchwood & 4th, a two-minute walk. We've got a bike rack right by the front door if you're rolling in on two wheels.

What to Expect

Ember & Oak is built for lingering. Think reclaimed wood tables, low light from the hearth, and the low hum of good conversation — not white tablecloths and hushed voices.

Cozy, Rustic Seating

A mix of two-tops, booths, and one long communal table near the oven — great for solo diners, date nights, or a group that wants to spread out and share.

Communal Table

Our 12-foot oak table by the fire seats strangers-turned-neighbors most nights. Ask for it if you're up for good company and better bread.

Kid-Friendly, Genuinely

Crayons at the host stand, a kids' menu with wood-fired mini pizzas, and zero side-eye for a little noise. Booster seats and high chairs always on hand.

Dress Code: None

Come as you are — from work clothes to weekend flannel. We care more about your appetite than your outfit.

Private Events & Large Groups

Celebrating something? Our communal table seats up to 14, and for bigger gatherings we can open the back room for a half or full buyout — up to 40 guests. We've hosted rehearsal dinners, retirement parties, and one memorable 90th birthday that ended in a conga line around the oven.

Reach out at least two weeks out for the best date availability. We'll build a family-style menu around your group, wood-fired courses and all.

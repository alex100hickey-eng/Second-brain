Our Story

We opened Ember & Oak eight years ago in an old hardware store on Maple Street, with a hand-built brick hearth and a simple idea: pizza is better when it's shared. The oven still runs on split oak, the dough still rests for two days before it sees the fire, and the same regulars still wave from their usual booth. We're not fancy — we're just doing the simple things carefully, night after night, for the people who live down the block.

From the Oven

Margherita

San Marzano tomatoes, fresh mozzarella, basil torn at the last second, a drizzle of good olive oil. The one that started it all, still our most-ordered pie.

Soppressata

Spicy soppressata that curls and crisps at the edges, house-made hot honey, a scatter of Calabrian chili. Sweet heat, built for sharing — if you're willing to.

Roasted Mushroom

Cremini and oyster mushrooms roasted until deeply browned, taleggio, fresh thyme, a whisper of garlic cream. Earthy, rich, and quietly our chef's favorite.

The Oven Itself

At the heart of the dining room sits our hearth — hand-laid firebrick, fed all day with seasoned oak, running around 700 degrees at its hottest corner. It takes about ninety seconds to turn raw dough into a blistered, smoky crust, but it took our builder six weeks to get the dome shape right. We still tend it by hand, still listen for the crackle that tells us it's ready, and still think there's no shortcut worth taking when it comes to real fire and real wood.

Community Table

Ask for the long table by the window and you'll likely end up sitting near someone who's been coming since we opened. We run a standing Tuesday night where three families split five pizzas and swap stories, and a birthday club that gets a free round of garlic knots when they book with us. Whether it's a first date, a Little League team celebrating a win, or just a Wednesday that needed saving, we set the table family-style and let the night take its time.

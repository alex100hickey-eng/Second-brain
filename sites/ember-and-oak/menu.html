A Note on Our Dough & Fire

Our dough rests for 48 hours before it ever sees the oven — long enough to build flavor and the kind of open, chewy crumb that holds up to a hot fire without going stiff. The oven itself burns oak, split and stacked out back, running near 900 degrees on a good night. That heat is why a pizza takes less time to cook than it does to talk about, and why the char on the crust tastes like something more than flour and water. We didn't invent any of this. We just tend the fire, mind the dough, and try not to get in its way.

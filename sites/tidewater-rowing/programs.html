Three boats, one boathouse

Whether you've never touched an oar or you're chasing a Head of the Charles bid, there's a seat for you on the Tidewater dock. Here's how the programs break down.

Learn-to-Row: your first stroke

Everyone who's ever rowed a stroke started exactly where you are now — standing on a dock, wondering if the boat is really as tippy as it looks (it's not, once you know what you're doing). Learn-to-Row is our six-week on-ramp, built for adults who have never rowed or haven't been in a boat since a gym-class fitness unit decades ago.

Weeks 1–2: Land & Water Basics

Erg technique, boat safety, and your first flatwater strokes in a stable four with a coach in the boat with you the entire time.

Weeks 3–4: Building the Stroke

Catch, drive, finish, recovery — we break the stroke into parts you can actually feel, then string them back together.

Weeks 5–6: Rowing as a Crew

Move into an eight, learn to row in sync with seven other people, and finish with a casual "graduation" row down the Lafayette River.

Sessions run Tuesday and Thursday evenings, 6:00–7:30 PM, plus one Saturday morning session per session cycle. All equipment is provided — you bring water shoes, a water bottle, and a willingness to get a little wet. New cohorts start the first Tuesday of March, May, July, and September.

Masters Program: fitness, friends, flexible schedules

This is the heart of the club — rowers ages 22 to 78, none of us training for the Olympics, all of us better off for the hour we spend on the water together. Masters is organized by ability, not age, so you'll row with people who match your fitness and technique, not just your birth year.

Recreational Squad

Steady-state rows, technique-focused, low pressure. Great for returning rowers easing back in or anyone who wants a great workout without a training plan hanging over their head.

Fitness Squad

Structured workouts with some intensity — intervals, pieces, the occasional erg test — for members who want measurable progress but no regatta obligation.

Competitive-Curious Squad

A bridge group for masters rowers testing the waters (pun intended) before committing to the Competitive Team's regatta schedule.

Masters rowers set their own attendance goals — some come three mornings a week, others make it work around a travel-heavy job and row twice a month. Practices run Monday, Wednesday, Friday at 6:00 AM and Saturday at 8:00 AM, year-round, weather (and tide) permitting.

Competitive Team: regatta calendar & training blocks

For rowers who want to race — former collegiate athletes, ambitious masters, and Learn-to-Row grads who caught the bug. Training is periodized around our regatta calendar, with clear blocks so you always know whether you're building base fitness or sharpening for a race.

Winter Base (Dec–Feb)

Erg-heavy, high-volume training indoors, with technical tank sessions and strength work twice a week at the boathouse gym.

Spring Build (Mar–May)

Back on the water, race-pace pieces, and our first regatta: the Tidewater Head Race, which we host on our home stretch of the Elizabeth River.

Summer Race Season (Jun–Aug)

Peak racing: Richmond Sprints, the Head of the Occoquan, and the Mid-Atlantic Masters Regatta round out the calendar, with lineups posted two weeks ahead.

Practices are five days a week, 5:45–7:30 AM, with an additional Saturday session that alternates between long steady-state rows and full race simulations. Seat racing happens each block to keep lineups fair and competitive. This track asks more of your calendar — but the payoff is crossing a finish line with seven people who trust you completely.

Coaching staff & boathouse equipment

Our coaching staff is led by Head Coach Renata Alves, a former Old Dominion University rower and USRowing Level 3 certified coach who has run the program since 2016. She's supported by four assistant coaches, each specializing in one of our tracks, plus a rotating crew of volunteer safety launch drivers.

The fleet lives in our boathouse on the Elizabeth River: six Vespoli eights, four quads, eight doubles, and a dedicated stable of wide-set Learn-to-Row fours built for maximum stability. Ergs, a full weight room, and a heated tank for winter technique work round out the facility. All boats are inspected and re-rigged every season by our equipment committee, so you're never fighting your gear — just the current.

Which program is right for you?

Answer honestly — the water doesn't care about ego, and neither do we.

"I've never rowed before."

→ Learn-to-Row. Six weeks, small groups, a coach in the boat with you. No experience, no problem.

"I rowed in college but haven't touched an oar in 15 years."

→ Masters Program, Recreational Squad. Your body remembers more than you think. We'll help the rest come back.

"I want a hard workout, but I'm not trying to race anyone."

→ Masters Program, Fitness Squad. Structure and sweat, zero regatta pressure.

"I miss the feeling of a lineup and a finish line."

→ Competitive Team. Early mornings, real training blocks, and a regatta calendar to chase.

"I'm not sure yet — I just want to see what this is about."

→ Start with Learn-to-Row or a Masters trial week. Come try an erg session on us before you commit to anything.

Membership Tiers

Every membership includes real coaching from real rowers — no recorded videos, no guesswork. Choose the level of commitment that matches your season.

Learn-to-Row Intro

$225 / 6-week session

Built for total beginners. Small groups of six, stable coastal shells, and a coach in a launch boat the whole time. By week three you'll be rowing in a four without white-knuckling the gunwale.

  • Two sessions per week, weekday evenings or Saturday mornings
  • All equipment provided — you bring water and a willingness to get a little wet
  • Runs spring (April–May) and fall (September–October)

Masters Annual

$895 / year

For rowers who've got their stroke and want a home. Unlimited access to club boats, our full masters practice calendar, and a seat in the boathouse community that shows up rain or shine.

  • Coached practices five mornings a week, year-round
  • Access to singles, doubles, quads, and eights per skill level
  • Entry fees covered for two regional regattas per year

Competitive Full

$1,450 / year

Our high-performance track for athletes training toward national masters events or a return to collegiate-level racing. Priority boat assignments, video stroke analysis, and a coach who knows your split times better than you do.

  • Six-day training block with structured erg and water sessions
  • Priority seat racing and lineup selection for regattas
  • Discounted entry to Head of the Chesapeake and regional sprints

How to Join

  1. Send an inquiry. Use our contact page to tell us your experience level and what you're hoping to get out of rowing — fitness, racing, or just quiet mornings on the water.
  2. Take a discovery row. We'll schedule a free, no-pressure outing with a coach so you can feel a shell under you before committing to anything.
  3. Pick your tier. Based on your goals and schedule, we'll recommend Learn-to-Row, Masters Annual, or Competitive Full — no upselling, just what fits.
  4. Sign up and get fitted. Complete your membership paperwork, get sized for oars and a seat, and receive your dock fob.
  5. First launch. You're on the water within a week of joining, usually sooner if the tide's right.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need to own any gear?

No. We provide boats, oars, and life jackets. Bring athletic clothes that can get wet, a water bottle, and sunscreen — the Bay glare is no joke by July.

What fitness level do I need?

None to start. Rowing is low-impact and scales with you. Our Learn-to-Row sessions are paced for first-timers, and masters coaches adjust workouts for every fitness level, from "just getting off the couch" to marathon-trained.

What if I rowed in college and I'm rusty?

Very common, very welcome. Most returning rowers slot straight into Masters Annual after one evaluation row with a coach to reacquaint your body with the stroke.

How flexible is the schedule?

Learn-to-Row has set class times, but Masters and Competitive members can attend any coached practice on the calendar — early morning, evening, or weekend — whatever fits your work and family life.

What happens in bad weather?

Safety comes first. Practices move indoors to the erg room during lightning, high wind, or ice, and we always post schedule changes to the club Slack by 5:30am.

Can I pause or cancel my membership?

Annual memberships can be paused for medical or relocation reasons with a note to our office — we'll prorate your remaining months toward your return.

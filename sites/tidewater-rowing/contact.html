Come find us at the dock

We're tucked into a bend of the Lafayette River, five minutes from downtown Norfolk and a world away from traffic. Directions, parking, gear, and everything else you need for your first pull.

Find the boathouse

Look for the gray shingled boathouse with the green dock — GPS sometimes wants to send you to the wrong side of the river, so see the driving note below.

Getting to the launch dock

From I-64: take Exit 279 toward Norfolk/VA-406, follow Tidewater Drive south, then turn onto Colley Avenue. Where Colley dead-ends at the water, bear right onto the gravel access road — that's us, not the public ramp next door.

The floating dock is on the downstream side of the boathouse. Boat traffic (trailers, launches) has the right of way there during morning practice — walk the upper path if you're arriving after 6:30am.

Parking, gear storage & what to bring

Parking

Gravel lot behind the boathouse holds about 30 cars — first-come, first-served. Overflow parking is along the access road shoulder, but please leave the turnaround clear for trailers. Don't park in the marked slips reserved for the sailing club next door; they will have you towed, and they will not feel bad about it.

Gear storage

Members get a bay in the erg room and a labeled hook in the rigging shed for oars, blades, and life jackets. Boats live on slings in the main bay — new rowers, your coach will walk you through the carry and rack system in week one so nothing gets scratched (including you).

What to bring your first day

  • Snug-fitting layers — no loose shorts or drawstrings that can catch on the seat track
  • Shoes you don't mind getting wet (or none — most of our shells row bare-foot in the summer)
  • A water bottle and a hat; the river doesn't care what season it is
  • Sunscreen, always, even in March
  • A willingness to get a little damp and laugh about it

We provide life jackets, oars, and boats for every intro session — you don't need to own a thing to start.

Season hours & morning launch times

Spring/Summer (April–September)

First launch: 5:45am, Tuesday–Saturday
Evening recreational row: 6:00pm Tuesdays & Thursdays
Saturday learn-to-row sessions: 8:00am, dock opens 7:30am

Fall (October–November)

First launch: 6:30am, Tuesday–Saturday
Head-race season means variable Saturday schedules — check the members calendar or ask your captain before you drive out.

Winter (December–March)

Erg-room training: Monday, Wednesday, Friday, 6:00–7:15am
Water sessions when temps and ice allow, announced by 9pm the night before via the team text chain.

Not sure which group you belong in? That's exactly what the inquiry form above is for — tell us your experience and we'll point you to the right launch time.

Your notes app shouldn't need its own study guide.

Somewhere between the syllabus and week twelve, most note systems collapse into a junk drawer — forty-three untitled docs, three different apps, a highlighter graveyard. You spend more time hunting for what you wrote than learning it.

Inkling starts from a different assumption: notes should get more useful the more you take, not less. Every entry is timestamped, tagged to its class automatically, and quietly connected to the ideas around it — so review isn't archaeology. It's just scrolling down.

Four habits, built into the app.

No settings to configure. Inkling just works the way a good study partner would.

⚡

Quick Capture

Open to a blank line in under a second, even from a locked phone. Type, dictate, or snap a photo of the whiteboard — it all lands in the same place, organized by class and time.

🔗

Smart Linking

Mention a term you've defined before, and Inkling quietly draws the thread. By midterms, your notes on "mitosis" already point to every lecture, lab, and flashcard that touched it.

◐

Focus Mode

One tap hides everything but the cursor — no sidebar, no notifications, no tabs. Built for the 50 minutes you actually have to get the lecture down before it moves on.

↻

Study Recall

Inkling turns your own notes into spaced-repetition prompts automatically — no manual flashcards. Review five minutes a day and it resurfaces exactly what's fading.

Three steps. Every class, every day.

  1. 01

    Jot

    Capture the lecture as it happens — typed, handwritten, or spoken. No formatting decisions, no folder to pick. Just write.

  2. 02

    Link

    Inkling scans for repeated terms and concepts across your notes and connects them, building a map of the course without any tagging on your part.

  3. 03

    Review

    Open Study Recall the night before an exam and get a tight, ranked summary of what you've written — weighted toward what you've seen least.

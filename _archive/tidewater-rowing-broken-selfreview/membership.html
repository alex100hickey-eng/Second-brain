Membership Overview

Tidewater Rowing Club is member-owned and member-run, based out of our boathouse on Willoughby Spit with direct access to the Lynnhaven and lower Chesapeake. Every membership includes access to our fleet, storage for your gear, and a coaching staff that actually knows your name and your catch angle.

We keep three membership paths simple: a low-commitment term for people just learning the stroke, an annual membership for masters rowers who want year-round water time, and a competitive track for athletes training toward regattas. Family and student discounts apply across all three. Read on for the details, then come meet us at the dock.

Learn-to-Row Term

Built for total beginners. Two evening sessions a week in stable four- and eight-person shells, led by a certified coach who will get you comfortable with the stroke, the calls, and the water before you ever think about racing.

  • 12 on-water sessions
  • Small groups, max 4 rowers per coach

What's Included

Membership dues cover more than dock access. Here's what's actually included, no upsells:

Boats & Oars

Full use of our fleet — 8+, 4+, 4x, 2x, and singles from Vespoli and Empacher, plus a rack of Concept2 and Croker oars sized to fit you.

Coaching

US Rowing-certified coaches on every squad, from your first catch to your last stroke before a regional final. Video review sessions run monthly.

Storage

A dedicated oar rack and gear locker at the boathouse, so your kit stays dry and ready between sessions — no hauling equipment home.

Community

Season kickoff cookouts, a member Slack for finding a boat partner, and a boathouse that's open for coffee and stretching before every practice.

Family & Student Rates

Rowing is better with your people in the boat next to you. We've built in discounts to make that easier:

  • Family Rate: 15% off each additional Masters or Competitive Annual membership for immediate family members in the same household.
  • Student Rate: Masters Annual drops to $650/year with a valid full-time student ID — college students home for the summer welcome.
  • Youth Add-On: Rowers 14–18 can join our junior development squad for $450/year, run alongside our Learn-to-Row terms.
  • Referral Credit: Bring a friend who joins any annual tier and you both get $50 off your next renewal.

Member FAQs

Do I need to know how to swim?

Yes — we require all members to demonstrate they can swim 100 yards and tread water for 5 minutes before their first on-water session. It's a safety non-negotiable, not a fitness test.

What if I've never rowed before?

Start with a Learn-to-Row term. Most graduates move straight into the Masters squad afterward, and we'll credit your term fee toward your first annual membership if you sign up within 30 days.

Can I bring my own boat?

Absolutely. Private boat owners pay a reduced $420/year rack fee that includes storage, dock access, and coaching eligibility, without fleet-use privileges.

Is there a minimum fitness level?

No. Our Masters squad rows in ability-grouped boats, so a 45-minute steady-state row and a 6k erg piece are both represented on the water most mornings.

What's the cancellation policy?

Annual memberships can be paused for medical or relocation reasons with 30 days' notice — no cancellation fee, and we'll hold your spot for up to 6 months.

When can I start?

Learn-to-Row terms begin monthly March–October. Masters and Competitive memberships are rolling — you can join any week of the year.

Ready to Join?

The Chesapeake doesn't wait, and neither should you. Come down to the boathouse for a free trial row, meet the squad, and pick the membership that fits. We'll have a boat ready.

Why We Row

Camaraderie That Sticks

Eight rowers and a coxswain can't fake it — the boat tells the truth. That's why our crews become the kind of friends who show up at 5:45 a.m. without complaint, and who you'll find at the dockside coffee thermos long after practice ends.

Fitness With a View

Rowing works nearly every major muscle group without pounding your joints. Our members range from 22 to 74, and most say they're stronger now than they were a decade ago — with a sunrise over the Bay Bridge thrown in for free.

The Bay, Properly Known

We launch from our dock near Willoughby Spit into open Chesapeake water — past the crab pot floats, under gulls working the tide line. You'll learn this water the way watermen do: one stroke, one season at a time.

A Morning on the Water

5:45 AM — Rigging Up

Headlamps click on. Oars come off the rack. Someone's already got the coffee going in the boathouse while the coxswains check the wind against the flag.

6:15 AM — First Twenty Strokes

The launch call echoes across still water. By stroke ten, the boat finds its set. By stroke twenty, nobody's thinking about the alarm clock anymore.

7:00 AM — Dock Talk

Boats come in, shoulders are sore, and the debrief happens right there on the dock — half coaching notes, half breakfast plans.

Programs Snapshot

Learn-to-Row

Six weeks, on-land technique plus guided water sessions in stable coaching launches. No experience required — just a willingness to get a little wet and laugh at yourself the first time you catch a crab.

Learn More

Masters Program

For rowers 21 and up, from returning collegiate athletes to brand-new converts. Three morning practices a week, plus optional weekend regattas up and down the Atlantic coast.

Learn More

Competitive Team

Selective squads training for USRowing Masters Nationals and regional Head Races. Serious mileage, serious results, and a team that pushes each other every single practice.

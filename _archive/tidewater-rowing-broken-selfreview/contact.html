Find the Boathouse

Tidewater Rowing Club is based out of the old Crawford Bay boathouse at 1420 Riverside Landing, Portsmouth, VA 23704 — a converted 1940s net-repair shed with a wide dock and a view straight down the channel toward the Elizabeth River.

Driving from Norfolk / I-264

Take I-264 W across the Downtown Tunnel, exit onto Effingham St, then follow Water St south for eight blocks. Turn right onto Riverside Landing — the boathouse is the gray building at the end of the lane, past the old crab shack.

Driving from Chesapeake / I-464

Take I-464 N to Portsmouth Blvd, follow signs for High St, then continue onto Water St. Riverside Landing is on your left just before the road curves toward the shipyard.

By bike or on foot

The Elizabeth River Trail passes within a quarter mile of the boathouse. Look for the blue Tidewater Rowing sign where the trail spur splits toward the water — we've got a bike rack by the side door.

Dock & Parking Details

Parking lot

The gravel lot behind the boathouse holds about 25 cars. It fills up fast for the 5:45am rows, so carpool when you can — regulars usually post a ride-share thread in the club group chat the night before.

Overflow parking

When the main lot's full, street parking along Riverside Landing is open to the public and free before 8am. Please don't block the driveway of the sail loft next door — they're early risers too.

The dock itself

Our floating dock has two launch bays and enough rack space for the whole fleet. It's steady underfoot but can get slick with dew — water shoes or an old pair of sneakers with grip are your friend.

Accessibility

The boathouse entrance and dock ramp are both wheelchair accessible with a gentle grade. Accessible parking is available directly beside the side door — just let us know ahead of time and we'll have it clear.

First Visit Checklist

Arrive 15 minutes early

Give yourself time to find parking, meet your coach, and fill out a one-page waiver. We'll get you a loaner life jacket and a quick tour of the boathouse.

Wear the right layers

Snug-fitting athletic clothes — no loose shorts or drawstrings that could snag on the seat track. Bring a warm layer for before and after; the water's cooler than the parking lot most mornings.

Bring water and shoes you don't mind getting wet

Old sneakers or water shoes are ideal. Leave the good running shoes at home — the dock and shells get damp, especially at sunrise.

Skip the big breakfast

A light snack and coffee are plenty for an early row. Save the full breakfast for after — plenty of our members swing by Baker's Crust on Water St. afterward.

No experience needed

Every Learn-to-Row session starts on land with the stroke basics before anyone touches the water. Nobody expects you to know port from starboard yet.

Say hello to Dana at check-in

Our membership coordinator will be at the dock most first mornings to make introductions and answer any last-minute questions.
